Pork Chops

A skillet of Fennel-Rubbed Pork Chops with Apple, Kale, and Sweet Potato

Ingridients

  • One teaspoon fennel seeds
  • Half teaspoon whole black peppercorns
  • Three-quarters teaspoon salt, divided
  • Four (1-inch thick) boneless pork loin chops
  • Quarter cup olive oil, divided
  • Half onion, cut thinly into half-moons
  • One red apple - unpeeled, cored, and sliced into quarter-inch-thick wedges
  • One cup reduced-sodium chicken broth
  • One teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Four cups lightly packed, coarsely chopped kale

Steps

  1. Place fennel seeds and peppercorns in a small zip-top plastic bag
  2. Seal bag and pound spices using a mallet or rolling pin until coarsely ground, or pulse them briefly in a spice or coffee grinder
  3. Sprinkle mixture and 1/4 teaspoon salt on both sides of chops
  4. Heat 2 tablespoons oil in a large, high-sided skillet over medium heat
  5. Add sweet potatoes; cook, stirring occasionally, until fork-tender, 10 to 12 minutes
  6. Sprinkle with 1/4 teaspoon salt, then transfer to a plate
  7. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in the same skillet over medium-high heat
  8. Add chops and reduce heat to medium; cook, turning once, about 4 minutes, or until an instant-read thermometer inserted into thickest parts registers 145 degrees F (63 degrees C)
  9. Transfer chops to a plate and tent with foil
  10. Add remaining 1 tablespoon oil and the onion to the skillet; cook until onion softens, about 2 minutes
  11. Add apple; cook 1 minute more. Add broth, stirring to scrape up browned bits, then stir in mustard and sweet potatoes
  12. Increase heat to medium-high; simmer until apple is slightly softened and liquid has slightly reduced, 1 to 2 minutes
  13. Add kale and remaining 1/4 teaspoon salt; cook, stirring occasionally, until kale is tender, about 1 minute
  14. Add any accumulated juices from the plate with pork. Serve chops with vegetable mixture
